Archive Structure

The archive intentionally avoids systems of central verification such as “correct,” “false,” “supported,” or “disputed.” Scientific reports, oral histories, sensory observations, children’s descriptions, environmental recordings, and speculative interpretations are preserved together as parallel knowledge structures. Contradictions are maintained rather than resolved.
